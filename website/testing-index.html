Dealing With Exam Stress

The Basics of Success

Keep your work organised
Use a timetable?

It's important to ensure all your work is kept organised and easy for you to find. This saves you time having to look for all your work. Almost everyone advises to create an exam timetable, which is essential to keeping organised. It helps you to know what you’re studying and when to take breaks. This even saves you time from having to pick what to study and what to priorities when.

TIP: Try easier subjects or modules at the beginning of your study sessions and slowly build in complexity.

Going bed early and
Getting sleep

It's important to sleep at least 8-10 hours every single night. Sleeping is better than staying up and studying all night. It's important not revise under the duvet - your bed is a sanctuary, not a desk! Not getting enough sleep could lead to many effects such as moodiness or even weaken your immune system which could add to your stress. Pulling constant all-nighter isn’t a great idea, it leads to worse effects of sleep deprivation and in fact you’ll be making the stress worse having no sleep.

Keep your health up
Loads of water and food

Nourish your body with lots of healthy grub. Fuel your body and mind with lots of greens, fruit, nuts and seeds for healthy energy boosting snacks and make sure you have a balanced diet of three meals a day. The NHS states that "the European Food Safety Authority recommends that women should drink about 1.6 litres of fluid and men should drink about 2.0 litres of fluid per day".

Talking to others helps
Friends, Parents and Teachers

It is easier to talk to others about what you're stressing over and you'll find that you’re not the only one that’s stressing with exams. Speaking to friends and taking breaks with them to do something other than studying could refresh your mind so when you sit to work, everything will seem easier. Speaking to your teachers and explaining your situation to them could help get you extensions on your deadlines, I mean, they want you to be successful as much as you want to yourself. Speaking to your parents about your exams and your timetable could help them decide when to give you space or help encourage you. There’s nothing wrong with reaching out to others for help.

Take Breaks
Go out! Meet friends! Have fun!

Be sensible with your work load and make sure you take a reasonable amount of breaks. Taking breaks between work sessions is essential to keep on track! These can be periodic 15 minute breaks every 45 minutes where you get small snacks to keep yourself energised. Or it can be a whole day break where you can spend the day with friends or family. You could always go cinema with them or play sports or even simply go for a stroll in the local park, whatever it is, just make sure you don’t think about work and exams! Additionally, don’t be afraid to take more than one break, if you feel overwhelmed with your workload take a few minutes on your break so when you return to work again you’re confident that you can start concentrating!

TIP: Try doing a different subject after each break! This will keep your brain active and you won't get bored as quick!

Acceptance and
Putting 100% effort

It sounds weird but many students spend more of their energy worrying about their exams rather than actually doing any preparations towards them. It’s important to just accept the things that you can’t change, your exams are coming and they will happen, nothing can change that but they will be over. So instead of wasting energy on worrying about them, focus your energy on the things you can control such as your revision timetable, working to achieve your goals but ultimately remember to give your 100% on the day of you exams and stay optimistic.

Believe in yourself and work harder!
